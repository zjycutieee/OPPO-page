Omni-Perception Policy Optimization for Multimodal Emotion Reasoning

Reliable multimodal emotion reasoning requires both sufficient perception and faithful grounding. Existing emotion Omni-MLLMs may ignore fine-grained visual cues or infer missing visual evidence from audio. OPPO targets these two failure modes through perception-aware reinforcement learning and evaluates them with MEP-Bench.

Abstract

Multimodal emotion reasoning asks an Omni-MLLM to identify emotions and explain them with visual, acoustic, and linguistic evidence. In practice, a fluent explanation is not always a reliable one: a model can overlook important cues, or describe facial and vocal evidence that is not actually supported by the corresponding modality. This work studies that gap from the perspective of omni-modal perception.

OPPO introduces a reinforcement learning framework that makes perception an explicit optimization target. The reward side encourages the reasoning trajectory to recover fine-grained evidence cues, while the loss side checks whether modality-specific tokens remain grounded when visual or audio inputs are masked. Together, these two components guide the model toward richer cue usage and fewer cross-modal hallucinations.

To diagnose this behavior, we build MEP-Bench, a benchmark focused on cue utilization and faithfulness under unimodal masking. Experiments on MER-UniBench show that improving grounded perception also improves downstream emotion reasoning performance.

Overview

Overview of OPPO. The framework first organizes ground-truth reasoning into video cues, audio cues, and emotion cues. During optimization, generated reasoning clauses are matched to these cues to reward evidence coverage. In parallel, unimodal masking creates counterfactual inputs so that modality-specific evidence tokens can be tested for whether they truly depend on the corresponding visual or acoustic signal.

Method

Omni-Perception Reward

The reward measures how completely the model recovers human-recognizable evidence in its reasoning. Ground-truth explanations are decomposed into atomic cues, and the generated chain of thought is split into short clauses. OPPO embeds both sides and builds an evidence-routing matrix that links each cue to the clause that best supports it.

This makes reward feedback fine-grained rather than label-only. Visual behaviors such as gaze and posture, acoustic patterns such as tone and rhythm, and emotion-level inferences are all counted separately, so the model is encouraged to use diverse multimodal evidence before producing the final emotion prediction.

Omni-Perception Loss

The loss focuses on whether a modality-specific statement is faithful to its source. OPPO compares the model distribution under the full input with the distribution under a counterfactual input where one modality is masked. If a visual cue is mentioned when the video is absent, or an audio cue survives without audio, the model is pushed away from that hallucinated evidence.

Unlike a global masking penalty, the KL term is applied only to tokens that describe the masked modality. This targeted design preserves general reasoning fluency while directly penalizing cross-modal hallucination on the evidence tokens that matter most for faithful emotion reasoning.

Benchmark

MEP-Bench is designed as a diagnostic benchmark for reliable perception in multimodal emotion reasoning. Instead of only checking whether the final emotion label is correct, it asks whether the reasoning process uses the right evidence and whether modality-specific claims remain grounded in the modality that supports them.

Source and Scale

MEP-Bench is derived from OV-MERD by selecting cue-rich samples where visual and acoustic evidence can be explicitly inspected. The benchmark contains 300 samples and constructs 500 probe questions for each masked modality evaluation.

Utilization

Utilization measures whether a model covers human-annotated multimodal cues in its generated reasoning. Human annotations and model thoughts are decomposed into atomic cues, embedded, and matched by cosine similarity. A cue is counted as recalled when its best generated counterpart passes the similarity threshold.

Faithfulness

Faithfulness is tested with POPE-style unimodal masking. For example, after masking video, the benchmark asks whether the video shows a specific expression. A correct refusal indicates that the model does not hallucinate visual evidence from audio or language priors.

Diagnostic Value

The two axes expose different failure modes. A model can mention too few cues even when its answer is plausible, or it can mention many cues while some are not grounded. MEP-Bench separates these cases through evidence recall, Acc@V, and Acc@A.

Main Results

Main results on MER-UniBench. OPPO achieves the best mean score among compared Omni-MLLM baselines, with consistent improvements across sentiment analysis, basic emotion recognition, and fine-grained emotion recognition. These results suggest that optimizing perception quality can translate into stronger final emotion predictions.

Qualitative Comparison

Qualitative comparison. OPPO retrieves richer multimodal evidence during reasoning and avoids hallucinating modality-specific cues under masking. Compared with the baseline, the generated explanations are more tightly grounded in visible facial behavior, acoustic cues, and emotion-relevant evidence.
