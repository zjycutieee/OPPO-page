OPPO improves multimodal emotion reasoning by encouraging models to use enough evidence and stay faithful to the modality that supports each claim.

Abstract

Multimodal emotion reasoning asks a model to infer emotions while grounding its explanation in visual, acoustic, and linguistic evidence. Existing emotion-oriented Omni-MLLMs can give plausible reasoning, but they often miss fine-grained cues or borrow evidence from the wrong modality. OPPO addresses these failures by optimizing perception itself rather than relying only on final-answer supervision.

OPPO combines an Omni-Perception Reward, which encourages generated reasoning to cover annotated multimodal evidence, with an Omni-Perception Loss, which checks whether visual and acoustic claims remain grounded under unimodal masking. We also introduce MEP-Bench to separately diagnose evidence utilization and modality faithfulness, and show that stronger perception leads to better performance on MER-UniBench.

Method Overview

OPPO consists of two tightly coupled objectives. The upper branch in the figure constructs a cue set from annotated video, audio, and emotion evidence, aligns generated reasoning clauses with those cues through an evidence-routing matrix, and turns the alignment into the Omni-Perception Reward. The lower branch masks one modality at a time and applies the Omni-Perception Loss to modality-specific evidence tokens, reducing visual or acoustic hallucination while preserving the full reasoning trajectory.

Qualitative comparison. OPPO retrieves richer multimodal evidence during reasoning and avoids hallucinating modality-specific cues under masking. Compared with the baseline, the explanations are more tightly grounded in visible facial behavior, acoustic cues, and emotion-relevant evidence.

Quantitative Results

MEP-Bench Diagnostic Benchmark

MEP-Bench is derived from OV-MERD and evaluates whether reasoning uses human-annotated multimodal cues and whether visual or acoustic claims remain faithful under unimodal masking. It separates underutilization from cross-modal hallucination through evidence recall and masked perception accuracy.

Main Results on MER-UniBench

OPPO achieves the best mean score among compared Omni-MLLM baselines, with consistent improvements across sentiment analysis, basic emotion recognition, and fine-grained emotion recognition.
